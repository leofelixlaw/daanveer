Mission to make a smile

2020 has been an unprecedented year. The Covid-19 pandemic raged, taking away precious lives and impacting livelihoods like never before. In India, millions lost their loved ones, their jobs and homes and struggled to manage one square meal a day. The physical, mental and emotional toll it has taken on the nation has been debilitating.

Beginning to serve a Pandemic-struck nation

Khushroo Poacha, serial socialpreneur and founder of Seva Kitchen and Indian Blood Donors, rose to the occasion once again. Through his brainchild, Seva Kitchen, meals and meal kits for the needy were crowdsourced with the support of donors from across the country. From migrant workers to farmer widows, orphanages, coolies at railway stations, poor patients & their families at hospitals and more, Seva Kitchen's initiatives touched the lives of thousands. Such was the scale of impact, that the work done, came to the attention of the Hon'ble Chief Minister of Maharashtra, Shri Uddhav Thackeray, who personally called Khushroo to acknowledge and appreciate his efforts. This greatly encouraged Khushroo to continue to serve the people and the nation.

How Daan Veer Works

A request comes in --> Details & payment information of the recipient is broadcast via Whatsapp to the daan veer community) --> The giver donates directly to the recipient's bank account and shares a screenshot of the payment amount --> Daan Veer shares updates of the fulfilled request and the total crowdfunded amount.

Daan Veer's journey has just begun. It is growing each day, with the support of people who are discovering the joy of giving. Rs. 50, 100 or 200 - no amount is too small. Time and time again, we have seen that micro giving can make a macro impact. As an individual you can make a difference together we can make a change.

 

Daan Veer is born

That was the beginning of Daan Veer, a Whatsapp initiative, focused around the idea of anonymous giving, with dignity. Through collective action by a group of voluntary donors, Daan Veer has reached out to support individuals from different walks of life. A Gond artist who had not sold a single painting since the beginning of the year, a man who needed funds to buy medicines for his ailing mother, a farm widow who was left almost penniless and many others.

Beginning to serve a Pandemic-struck nation

A phone call from a stranger, made Khushroo realize that there was a calling to do even more. A man from Thane, Mumbai called, saying that his family was hungry and had not eaten in days. In the days that followed, calls came from desperate individuals. Khushroo's phone number had providentially been added as the State Helpline contact number of multiple states. Khushroo says 'When I heard these stories, at first, I didn't know why they were calling me and how I could help them. But their troubles kept me up at night.
